About

Who am I?

Hi! I’m Paul Poleon Jr — an Industrial Engineering student at the University at Buffalo with an interest in solving problems, improving systems, and making things work better in the real world.

I’m naturally curious about how things operate — whether that’s a workflow, a team dynamic, or a real-world process — and I love turning that curiosity into practical improvements.

Background

How did I get into Industrial Engineering?

I didn’t start in Industrial Engineering — I originally began in Mechanical Engineering. I enjoyed the technical side, especially 3D modeling, but a big shift happened when I worked as an Advanced Repair Agent with Geek Squad.

In that role, I wasn’t just fixing devices. I was diagnosing real problems, communicating clearly with customers, managing time, and finding practical solutions under pressure.

That experience made something click: I was most interested in how systems and people work together — not just the mechanics. Switching to Industrial Engineering ended up being one of the best decisions I’ve made, and I’ve been more engaged and motivated ever since.

Motivation

What do I like working on?

I enjoy breaking down complex problems, analyzing data, and building improvements that make a real difference. I’m especially drawn to work where the solution has to be both technically sound and realistic for the people using it.

Through coursework and projects, I’ve built skills in process mapping, data analysis, and continuous improvement — and I’m excited to apply those skills in internships and real-world settings.

Leadership

Where did I learn leadership and teamwork?

Outside of academics, I really value leadership, teamwork, and connection with others. I earned my Eagle Scout rank after years of dedication and service, and I served as Senior Patrol Leader for many years.

Leading my troop meant organizing activities, working closely with both scouts and adult leaders, and learning how to bring different personalities together toward a shared goal. Those experiences strengthened my communication, responsibility, and ability to collaborate — and they’ve translated directly into how I work in engineering teams.

Life

What am I like outside of class?

Sports have played a big role in my life — I played football as a lineman and lacrosse as a goalie. Those positions taught me to see the bigger picture, anticipate problems before they happen, and stay calm under pressure.

After high school, I came back to help coach, and I loved mentoring players, building teamwork, and helping others grow both on and off the field. I’m still a huge football fan — I love watching games, debating plays, and keeping up with stats and highlights. Come Sunday, you’ll probably find me at a tailgate or glued to a TV screen.

When I’m not studying or watching football, I’m usually hanging out with friends or playing video games. I enjoy the strategy and competition, and I think they’ve helped sharpen my ability to think critically and adapt quickly.

Above all, I’m a very social person who thrives in team environments with open communication. Technical skills matter — but I believe the ability to work well with others is what really separates a good engineer from a great one.
