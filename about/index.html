About

Who am I?

Hi! I’m Paul Poleon Jr — an Industrial Engineering student at the University at Buffalo focused on improving systems and making work run better.

Background

How did I get here?

I didn’t always start out in Industrial Engineering — I originally began in Mechanical Engineering. I enjoyed the technical side, especially 3D modeling, but my time as an Advanced Repair Agent with Geek Squad reshaped my perspective. In that role, I was diagnosing real problems, communicating with customers, managing time, and finding practical solutions under pressure. That experience helped me realize I was more drawn to how systems and people work together than just the mechanics of things.

Motivation

What drives me?

I’ve always been curious about how things operate — workflows, team dynamics, and real-world processes. I enjoy breaking down complex problems, analyzing data, and building practical improvements that stick. Through coursework and projects, I’ve developed skills in process mapping, analysis, and continuous improvement, and I’m excited to apply them in internships and real-world settings.

Leadership

Where did I learn to lead?

I earned my Eagle Scout rank and served as Senior Patrol Leader for years. Leading my troop taught me responsibility, communication, and how to work with different personalities — skills that translate directly to collaborative engineering work.

Life

What am I like outside of class?

Sports shaped how I think about teamwork and pressure — I played football (lineman) and lacrosse (goalie). Those roles trained me to see the bigger picture, anticipate problems early, and communicate quickly. I also coached after high school, which I loved because it let me mentor others and build team culture.
